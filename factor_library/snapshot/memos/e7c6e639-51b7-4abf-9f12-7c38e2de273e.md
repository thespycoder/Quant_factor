## Interpretation

The factor's verdict of 'not significant' suggests that the observed relationship between uncertainty language and future performance is not robust enough to be relied upon. Furthermore, the factor failed to meet the novelty check, indicating that the apparent relationship may be explained by existing risk factors rather than a genuine, novel effect. The smooth decay shape of the factor's signal also implies that the apparent edge is not concentrated in a particular horizon, but the factor's lack of significance and novelty raises doubts about its reliability.

## Recommendation

Given the factor's lack of significance and failure to meet the novelty check, we do not recommend using the uncertainty sentiment ratio factor as-is. While the underlying idea of capturing shifts in sentiment through language analysis is worth exploring, the current implementation does not appear to be a reliable leading indicator of future performance, and further modifications or re-evaluations may be necessary to determine its potential usefulness.
