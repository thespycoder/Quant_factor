# Factor Research Memo: uncertainty_to_positive_sentiment_ratio

## Summary

This factor, uncertainty_to_positive_sentiment_ratio, measures the balance between language of uncertainty and positivity in a company's annual 10-K filings, with the idea that a higher ratio may signal management's attempts to temper overly optimistic expectations.

## Hypothesis & Rationale

- **Description:** Calculates the ratio of uncertainty language to positive sentiment language in a company's 10-K filing
- **Computation:** ratio(`lm_category:LM_uncertainty`, `lm_category:LM_positive`), normalized by filing length
- **Direction:** -1    **Horizon:** fwd_ret_63d    **Universe:** all
- **Economic rationale:** A higher ratio of uncertainty to positive language may indicate management's attempts to temper optimistic expectations, which can be a sign of impending negative surprises; as a result, a higher ratio predicts lower forward returns.
