# Factor Research Memo: litigation_sentiment

## Summary

The litigation sentiment factor captures the relative frequency of litigious language versus positive sentiment in 10-K filings, aiming to identify companies that may be preparing for or responding to legal challenges.

## Hypothesis & Rationale

- **Description:** Captures the relative frequency of litigious language versus positive sentiment in 10-K filings
- **Computation:** ratio(`lm_category:LM_litigious`, `lm_category:LM_positive`), normalized by filing length
- **Direction:** -1    **Horizon:** fwd_ret_63d    **Universe:** all
- **Economic rationale:** A higher ratio of litigious to positive language may indicate that management is preparing for or responding to legal challenges, which can lead to increased costs, reputational damage, and decreased investor confidence, ultimately negatively impacting future returns.

## Interpretation

The factor was deemed not statistically significant, and it failed to demonstrate novelty. This suggests that the apparent effect of litigation sentiment on returns may be concentrated in a particular horizon or driven by existing risk factors, rather than a persistent and unique influence. A smooth decay shape indicates that the factor's predictive power does not diminish over time, but this finding is tempered by the lack of statistical significance and novelty.

## Recommendation

Given the factor's failure to clear the significance and novelty gates, we do not recommend using it in its current form. However, the underlying idea of capturing litigious language in 10-K filings may still be worth revisiting in modified form, potentially incorporating additional data sources or refining the methodology to better isolate the effect of interest.
