# Factor Research Memo: litigious_language_density

## Summary

This factor, litigious_language_density, measures the extent to which a company's 10-K filing contains language that suggests an increased likelihood of costly legal disputes. The hypothesis is that management's use of such language is a signal for potential future returns to be lower.

## Hypothesis & Rationale

- **Description:** Measures the density of litigious language in a company's 10-K filing
- **Computation:** sum(`phrase:litigation`), normalized by filing length
- **Direction:** -1    **Horizon:** fwd_ret_21d    **Universe:** all
- **Economic rationale:** Management's increased use of litigious language may indicate a higher likelihood of costly legal disputes, which can negatively impact future returns; as a result, higher litigious language density predicts lower forward returns.

## Interpretation

The factor's verdict indicates that it failed to demonstrate statistically significant predictive power. Moreover, the factor's lack of novelty suggests that its apparent edge may be merely a reflection of already-known risk factors, rather than a genuine, previously unexploited signal. The smooth decay shape further supports this interpretation, as it implies that the factor's predictive power is not concentrated in any particular horizon.

## Recommendation

Given the factor's failure to clear the significance and novelty gates, we do not recommend using it in its current form. However, the underlying idea of litigious language density as a potential signal for costly legal disputes may still be worth revisiting, perhaps with modifications to the signal construction or the inclusion of additional variables to enhance its predictive power.
